Courses

The Neural Networks Lab (NNLab) is actively involved in teaching and mentoring across a range of courses spanning machine learning, computer vision, and multimodal AI. Our courses are designed to balance theoretical foundations with hands-on, real-world applications.

Introduction to Machine Learning

This course provides a foundational understanding of machine learning concepts, algorithms, and practical implementation.

Topics Covered

  • Supervised vs Unsupervised Learning
  • Linear and Logistic Regression
  • Decision Trees and Ensemble Methods
  • Model Evaluation (Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1)
  • Overfitting and Regularization
  • Feature Engineering and Data Preprocessing
  • Introduction to Neural Networks
  • Hands-on with Python and Scikit-learn

Deep Learning

This course focuses on neural networks and modern deep learning architectures used in real-world applications.

Topics Covered

  • Fundamentals of Neural Networks
  • Backpropagation and Optimization
  • Activation Functions and Loss Functions
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)
  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs, LSTMs, GRUs)
  • Transfer Learning and Fine-tuning
  • Model Regularization Techniques (Dropout, BatchNorm)
  • Training at Scale (GPUs, Distributed Training)

Computer Vision

This course explores techniques for understanding and interpreting visual data using classical and deep learning methods.

Topics Covered

  • Image Processing Fundamentals
  • Feature Detection and Matching
  • Object Detection and Localization
  • Image Classification
  • Semantic and Instance Segmentation
  • Video Understanding and Tracking
  • Vision Transformers (ViTs)
  • Real-world Applications (Autonomous Driving, Healthcare)

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

This course introduces methods for processing and understanding textual data.

Topics Covered

  • Text Preprocessing and Tokenization
  • Language Modeling
  • Word Embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe)
  • Sequence Models (RNNs, LSTMs)
  • Attention Mechanisms
  • Transformers and BERT-like Models
  • Text Classification and Sentiment Analysis
  • Question Answering and Text Generation

Multimodal Machine Learning

Advanced

This advanced course focuses on integrating multiple data modalities such as image, text, and audio.

Topics Covered

  • Multimodal Representation Learning
  • Cross-modal Retrieval (Image ↔ Text)
  • Fusion Techniques (Early, Late, Hybrid Fusion)
  • Vision-Language Models (VLMs)
  • Contrastive Learning (CLIP-style models)
  • Multimodal Alignment and Translation
  • Handling Missing Modalities
  • Applications in Healthcare, Retail, and Robotics

Advanced Topics in AI Systems

Advanced

This course emphasizes system design, scalability, and deployment of AI models.

Topics Covered

  • Model Optimization and Compression
  • Efficient Architectures for Edge Deployment
  • Real-time Inference Systems
  • Data Pipelines and Batch Processing
  • MLOps and Model Lifecycle Management
  • Cloud Deployment (AWS, Azure, GCP)
  • Monitoring and Debugging AI Systems
  • Responsible AI and Ethics

Research Methods in AI

This course prepares students for conducting research in machine learning and AI.

Topics Covered

  • Reading and Analyzing Research Papers
  • Problem Formulation in AI
  • Experimental Design and Ablation Studies
  • Benchmarking and Evaluation Protocols
  • Writing Research Papers
  • Reproducibility in AI Research
  • Peer Review and Presentation Skills

Industrial Applications of AI

This course bridges the gap between academic research and real-world deployment.

Topics Covered

  • AI in Healthcare (Diagnosis, Imaging)
  • AI in Retail (Recommendation Systems, Visual Search)
  • Industrial Monitoring and Safety Systems
  • Smart Cities and Surveillance
  • Human-AI Interaction
  • Case Studies and Industry Projects

Capstone Project / Lab

A hands-on course where students apply their knowledge to real-world problems under faculty guidance.

Topics Covered

  • Problem Definition and Proposal
  • Dataset Collection and Annotation
  • Model Design and Implementation
  • Evaluation and Iteration
  • Deployment (Optional)
  • Final Report and Presentation

Teaching Philosophy

  • ✦Conceptual clarity over rote learning
  • ✦Hands-on experimentation and reproducibility
  • ✦Bridging theory with real-world applications
  • ✦Encouraging research and innovation
